Tax Services

Tax Preparation & Planning

Returns that get filed correctly the first time, and planning conversations that happen long before April.

Less scrambling, more strategy

Most firms treat tax season as a once-a-year event. We treat it as the final step in a year-round conversation. Whether you're an individual with a complicated return, a business owner managing quarterly estimates, or an executive navigating equity compensation, the goal is the same: no surprises when the return is finally filed.

Matthew Russell leads the practice personally, which means the person reviewing your return in March is the same person you talked strategy with in September.

What's included

  • Tax Return Preparation
    • Individuals
    • Non-public Businesses
    • Nonprofit Organizations
    • Estates and Trusts
  • Information Returns
  • Payroll, personal property, and sales tax returns
  • Business licenses and annual reports
  • Tax Planning

How an engagement starts

1.

Initial review

We look at your last two returns and current situation to flag anything worth addressing before it becomes urgent.

2.

Planning conversation

A sit-down (or video call) to talk through goals — minimizing liability, timing income, structuring a sale, whatever's relevant.

3.

Ongoing relationship

Quarterly or as-needed check-ins so filing season is a formality, not a fire drill.
